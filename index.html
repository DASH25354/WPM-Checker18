【18】Japan has about 4.4 million companies. The number of women company leaders reached a record: 684,669, up 5.4% from the previous year. This is an increase of 35,407 from last year. Women now make up 15.55% of all leaders, compared with 15.24% before. In 2010, there were 212,153 women leaders. Over 15 years, the number grew about 3.2 times. By prefecture, Tokyo has the most women leaders at 175,258, and Shimane has the fewest at 1,768, showing a very large gap. Okinawa has a rate of 20.6%, the only area over 20%. There are actions to support women: the government supports start-ups and business handover, and companies and universities join these efforts. These steps are slowly bringing results. More women leaders can help local economies and ease succession problems as the population ages and births fall. However, old ideas about women’s roles remain. Balancing work, housework, child care, and elder care needs support and understanding. Governments and local bodies should give business support and also help with child care, elder care, and social awareness changes linked to low birth rate issues.
